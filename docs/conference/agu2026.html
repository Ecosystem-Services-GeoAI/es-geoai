Session Description

Human health and well-being are tied to ecosystem services—from food and water security to climate regulation and cultural benefits. In response to global initiatives such as UNEP TEEB, IPBES, the SDGs, and the 2050 Vision for Biodiversity, there is a growing need for integrated, data-driven assessments that make nature’s benefits more visible. This session invites interdisciplinary contributions that leverage Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI) for advancing biodiversity and ecosystem services research. We welcome studies on: (1) applications of GeoAI to quantify the supply, demand, and flows of ecosystem services using multimodal data sources, including but not limited to remote sensing, citizen science, mobile, and other crowdsourced data; (2) advancements in GeoAI methods (e.g., multimodal learning, foundation models, knowledge-informed machine learning); (3) model-data integration to evaluate and forecast spatio-temporal dynamics and interactions among multiple ecosystem services and biodiversity across scales; and (4) interpretation, quantification, and communication of uncertainties in GeoAI model predictions.
